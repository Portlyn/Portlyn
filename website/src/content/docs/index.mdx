Portlyn is one Go binary. It runs as a hub on a VPS, terminates TLS, and checks who's allowed in. An optional node agent runs on a machine behind your firewall and dials the hub over WireGuard, so nothing at home ever opens a port.

I got tired of wiring Traefik, Authelia, and WireGuard together every time I wanted to share one service. So I put the three jobs in one binary that a normal person can actually run.

## Why not just...

- **Cloudflare Tunnel?** Works, but your traffic runs through Cloudflare and the auth is theirs. Portlyn keeps both on your own box.
- **Nginx Proxy Manager?** Great proxy, no auth layer and no answer for the machine behind NAT.
- **Traefik + Authelia + WireGuard?** That's exactly the stack Portlyn replaces. Three moving parts become one.
- **Tailscale?** Different goal. Tailscale gets *you* onto the network. Portlyn puts a *public* service online with a login in front, for people who aren't on your tailnet.

## Sharing without accounts

Drop a PIN or a one-time email code in front of any route. Good enough to hand a link to a friend, no account for them to make.
